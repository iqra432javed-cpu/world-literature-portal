🦅 Chapter 8: Reading Indigenous Voices

Joy Harjo — Native American Identity, Memory, Cultural Inheritance

8.1

Remember — Joy Harjo (United States, Mvskoke Nation, 1983)

POETRY · HIGH YIELD
▼
Joy Harjo — First Native American US Poet Laureate (2019). Member of the Mvskoke (Creek) Nation. Her poetry blends Indigenous traditions with modern political consciousness.

Analysis of "Remember"

  • An imperative poem — each stanza begins with "Remember."
  • Calls the reader to remember their connections to the natural world: "Remember the sky that you were born under."
  • Reminds us of our connections to sun, moon, plants, animals, and ancestors.
  • Ends: "Remember that you are all people / and that all people / are you."

Themes

  • Native American Identity: Reclaiming indigenous connections to land and cosmos.
  • Memory and Cultural Inheritance: Memory as act of cultural survival.
  • Connection to Nature: Non-hierarchical relationship between humans and the natural world.
  • Decolonization: Resisting cultural erasure through remembering.
  • Universal Humanity: All people are interconnected — anti-individualist.

Indigenous Literary Tradition

  • Oral tradition: poetry was always spoken/performed before written.
  • Land is not property but a living relationship.
  • Ancestors exist in the present — not the past.
  • Joy Harjo also a saxophonist — music inseparable from poetry.
